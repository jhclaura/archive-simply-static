Tzina: Symphony of Longing<\/strong> is a WebVR documentary about people inhabited Tzina Dizengoff square, talking about their lives, love, and loneliness. The square was\u00a0demolished at the beginning of 2017.<\/p>\n

Created and directed by Shirin Anlen<\/a>, the project invites the viewer for a physical walk to explore the square, to visit and hear the characters, at different times of the day. It’s made by Shirin,\u00a0\u200bZiv Schneider<\/a>,\u00a0Or Fleisher<\/a>, \u200bAvner Peled<\/a>, and me. Read more here:\u00a0shirin.works\/tzina-symphony-of-longing<\/a><\/p>\n

I was invited to join the team in Summer 2016, to create the back-animation for\u00a0the character interviews, which were captured with DepthKit<\/a>. It’s a pleasant process to discuss with director Shirin, from her understanding of the people she interviewed and her visions, to my interpretation and visualization.<\/p>\n

For me, it’s an interesting yet challenging task to maintain the balance of interpretation. I learned how to intrigue viewers without overshadowing the\u00a0monologue, and am really happy with the result of magical feeling. I feel I might purposefully highlight the more lovely moment of each characters stories, and I hope I did a not-bad-job in the end to try to interpret their lives :)<\/p>\n
