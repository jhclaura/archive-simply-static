The third major experiment I had this summer was a street car journey. At first it’s just another experiment with Unity, but then\u00a0it\u00a0leads me to an idea for the project I want to do this year, as the Research Fellow at ITP.<\/p>\n

I decided to establish an incorporation, called WE ARE WHAT WE PRETEND TO BE Inc.<\/strong><\/em><\/span>.<\/p>\n

It’s inspired by the quotes of writer\u00a0Kurt Vonnegut<\/a>:<\/p>\n

“We are what we pretend to be, so we must be careful about what we pretend to be.”<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

I discovered\u00a0this when I was doing research for my thesis, and it inspired me a lot to shape the idea, and to make the MASK<\/a><\/span><\/strong> series.<\/p>\n

WE ARE WHAT WE PRETEND TO BE Inc.(WAWPRE<\/strong>) <\/em>is an entity for my series experiments about the relationship between our free will<\/strong>\u00a0and unfree (uncontrollable) will<\/strong>. Can I change my unfree will by change my free will? How can I shift self identity by ultering\u00a0my and also others perspective?<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

The first product of\u00a0WE ARE WHAT WE PRETEND TO BE Inc.<\/strong>\u00a0<\/em>is MASK<\/a><\/span><\/strong><\/span>, a customized VR headset that helps the user become what they want to be.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

The second service of\u00a0WE ARE WHAT WE PRETEND TO BE Inc.<\/em><\/strong>\u00a0is THE TRAM<\/strong><\/span>.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

It’s an installation that provided customized virtual reality experience that users (aka passengers) can design\u00a0their tram\u00a0journey based on their mood, destination, favorite color, and cloth\u00a0to wear. Also from the cutout hole on the cardboard box, which is usually covered with curtain, I will take a picture of the passengers, so\u00a0the\u00a0passengers will be able to see themselves in the VR.<\/p>\n

WE ARE WHAT WE PRETEND TO BE Inc. THE TRAM<\/em>\u00a0<\/strong>takes you to wherever you want, fulfill whatever desire you want, therefore you become whomever you want. You set yourself free.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

I joined Internet Yami-Ichi in New York<\/a><\/em> this summer, and displayed my little handcraft Home Depot cardboard box as an installation + experience seller. For the software, I used Unity and started developing it on and off since August, since I was notified that I was selected to join the event. As for the box, I made it with recycled materials I found in junk shelf in one night<\/strong><\/em>\u00a0before the event! Holy cow SO NERVOUS.<\/p>\n

It’s really a successful experiments, for first ever user test! The users used it for a long time, average at least more than 1 minute! Which is a huge difference for me. \u00a0I think that’s because the tram\u00a0keeps moving, the scenery outside the window keeps changing, plus there are ghost passengers getting on the bus when the tram\u00a0stops at the tram\u00a0station, the user is expecting things to happen, thus enjoys the VR experience for the longer time.
